### 🎯 Actuarial-Relevant Research

**Natural Catastrophe Losses & Insurance Protection Gaps — 2026 Update**
Summary: Global natural catastrophe insured losses have exceeded $100B USD for the sixth consecutive year (Aon, 2026). Severe convective storms (SCS) have overtaken tropical cyclones as the costliest peril for insurers in the 21st century. In 2025 alone, California wildfires caused ~$40B and SCS ~$50B in insured losses in the US. APRA's climate stress test projects Australian weather-related annual expected losses growing from A$7B to over A$16B by 2050. Traditional actuarial models based on historical data are failing; the industry is shifting to forward-looking climate scenario analysis.
Source: https://www.iais.org/2025/11/iais-report-examines-financial-stability-implications-from-natural-catastrophe-insurance-protection-gaps/
