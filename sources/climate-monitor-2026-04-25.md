## 📚 New Research

### 1. COP31 Climate Finance Implementation Challenge
**Title:** COP31 2026: The $1.3 Trillion Climate Finance Implementation Challenge Explained  
**Summary:** Overview of COP31 (Antalya, November 2026) as an implementation summit rather than another target-setting round. The core challenge is operationalizing the COP29/COP30 pathway from roughly $190B of current annual climate finance toward $1.3T per year for developing countries by 2035, including transparent allocation mechanisms and much larger public-private capital mobilization.  
**URL:** https://informedclearly.com/en/environment/48757/cop31-climate-finance-implementation-challenge-2026  
**Published:** 2026-04-18

### 2. Descartes: Parametric Climate Stress Insurance for Data Centers
**Title:** Parametric Climate Stress Insurance for Data Centers  
**Summary:** Descartes Underwriting describes a temperature-indexed parametric product for data centers covering extreme heat and cold. The structure emphasizes rapid, objective payouts for business interruption, non-damage business interruption (NDBI), and extra expense exposures, with limits up to $80M per contract and global availability.  
**URL:** https://descartesunderwriting.com/white-papers/parametric-climate-stress-insurance-data-centers  
**Published:** 2026-04

### 3. WRI-Cited Outlook: Six Sustainable Finance Opportunities in 2026
**Title:** 2026年全球可持续金融发展六大机遇：国际变局之下，如何应对挑战？  
**Summary:** A Sina ESG article citing the World Resources Institute (WRI) argues that 2026 sustainable-finance work should move from record clean-energy and green-bond volumes toward execution in emerging markets and developing economies. It highlights six opportunity areas for public and private investors, links the agenda back to the $1.3T climate-finance target, and stresses stronger international financial coordination despite softer ESG and public-finance conditions in some markets.  
**URL:** https://finance.sina.cn/esg/2026-04-13/detail-inhukeeu5508499.d.html  
**Published:** 2026-04-13
