### 2. SOA: Catastrophe Planning & Financial Modeling for Retirement
**Title:** Climate Risk: Catastrophe Planning and Financial Modeling for Retirement  
**Summary:** SOA research essay covering how insurance organizations estimate capital needs for catastrophe exposures (S&P estimates $21.7B required capital reserve), the risk of underestimating escalation due to inconsistent climate data, and how claims ratio analysis informs financial planning. Notes that January 2025 LA fires ($40B+ insured losses) and 2025 severe convective storm losses ($50B) demonstrate expanding catastrophe risk beyond tropical cyclones.  
**URL:** https://www.soa.org/globalassets/assets/files/resources/research-report/2026/ar208-essay-collection/2026-ar208-cohen-catastropheplanning.pdf  
**Published:** 2026

### 3. P&C Market Cycle Analysis 2026
**Title:** The P&C Market Cycle in 2026: From Hard Market to Segmented Softening  
**Summary:** Actuarial analysis noting that California wildfire has evolved from a secondary concern to a primary catastrophe peril. The $40B+ insured losses from January 2025 LA fires combined with $50B severe convective storm losses in 2025 demonstrate catastrophe risk extends well beyond hurricanes and earthquakes. Actuaries building catastrophe models focused primarily on hurricane and earthquake perils may be underweighting wildfire's growing contribution.  
**URL:** https://actuary.info/insights/pc-market-cycle-2026-actuarial-analysis  
**Published:** 2026

### 4. EIOPA: Insurance Protection Gaps in a Changing Climate
**Title:** Insurance protection gaps in a changing climate  
**Summary:** EIOPA raises the alarm about Europe's significant protection gap against natural disasters: only ~25% of nat-cat losses in the EU have been insured over the past decades. Historical data is no longer a reliable predictor of future losses as climate change increases frequency and severity of floods, droughts, storms, and wildfires. Calls for systemic solutions including public-private mechanisms.  
**URL:** https://www.eiopa.europa.eu/insurance-protection-gaps-changing-climate-2026-04-16_en  
**Published:** 2026-04-16

---

### 5. EIOPA + ESM: European NatCat Pool Mechanism
**Title:** EIOPA and ESM staff propose mechanism to better manage fallout from outsized natural catastrophes  
**Summary:** Joint EIOPA-ESM proposal for a European natural catastrophe pool to reduce Europe's protection gap (~75% of economic losses uninsured) and strengthen resilience against increasingly frequent and severe disasters. Primary insurers and reinsurers alone may not have sufficient capacity. The proposed mechanism would complement national schemes and market-based solutions.  
**URL:** https://www.eiopa.europa.eu/eiopa-and-esm-staff-propose-mechanism-better-manage-fallout-outsized-natural-catastrophes-2026-04-09_en  
**Published:** 2026-04-09
