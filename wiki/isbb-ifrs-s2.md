# ISSB IFRS S2 - Climate Disclosure Standard

> The ISSB's climate-related disclosure standard (IFRS S2) continues to evolve through targeted amendments, implementation guidance, and industry-based updates ahead of fuller supervisory adoption.

## Overview

IFRS S2 (Climate-related Disclosures) is the ISSB's flagship standard requiring companies to disclose climate-related risks and opportunities. The standard builds on TCFD recommendations and is being adopted globally. Recent 2026 materials show the work is no longer just about the core standard text: implementation questions such as financed emissions, biogenic emissions, and industry-based guidance are now central.

## Key Facts

- **Effective date:** January 1, 2027 (early adoption permitted)
- **Scope 3 Category 15 (financed emissions):** targeted simplifications remain especially important for banks, asset managers, and insurers
- **Biogenic emissions guidance:** March 2026 TIG paper adds specific interpretation guidance for applying IFRS S2 to biogenic CO2 emissions
- **Industry-based updates:** February 2026 exposure draft proposes enhancements to SASB Standards and IFRS S2 industry-based guidance
- **Assurance practice is maturing too:** 2026 audit guidance is now drilling into Scope 1/2/3 evidence, governance, reporting boundaries, and climate-control testing under the four IFRS S2 pillars
- **Nature-related Disclosures:** ISSB continues advancing a broader nature-related project aligned with TNFD, SASB, GRI, and ESRS
- **Insurance relevance:** disclosure expectations are becoming more detailed at exactly the points where insurers have financed-emissions and sector-specific judgment calls
