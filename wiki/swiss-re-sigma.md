# Swiss Re Institute — Nat Cat Loss Research

> Swiss Re Institute's sigma publications are the primary industry reference for global natural catastrophe loss data.

## 2025 Loss Data (sigma 1/2026, published March 19, 2026)

- **Global nat cat insured losses in 2025: US$107 billion**
- **92% driven by secondary perils** (wildfires, SCS, floods)
- **Wildfires** set a new loss record (Palisades + Eaton fires, Los Angeles: combined insured losses US$40 billion)
- **US$51 billion** in SCS losses — third costliest year on record for SCS
- Global protection gap: ~49% (record)

## 2026 Forecasts

| Scenario | 2026 Projection |
|----------|----------------|
| Long-term trend | US$148 billion |
| By 2030 | US$186 billion |
| Peak-loss scenario | US$320 billion |
| Extreme scenario by 2030 | US$400 billion |

> The growth is structurally driven by exposure accumulation, not weather volatility alone.
