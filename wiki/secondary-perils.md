# Secondary Perils & Nat Cat Loss Trends

> Secondary perils (wildfires, severe convective storms, floods) now dominate global natural catastrophe insured losses — structurally, not cyclically.

## Overview

The insurance industry's primary natural catastrophe loss driver has shifted from traditional primary perils (hurricanes, earthquakes) to secondary perils. This is a structural change, not a weather cycle: exposure accumulation in high-risk zones, land use change, and climate-driven weather anomaly intensification all contribute.

## Key Facts

- **2025 global nat cat insured losses: US$107 billion** — third costliest year on record
- **92% of 2025 losses driven by secondary perils** (wildfires, SCS, floods)
- **Severe convective storms (SCS) overtook tropical cyclones** as the costliest insurance peril of the 21st century
- **US SCS losses in 2025: US$51 billion** (third-highest on record); 30 individual billion-dollar events globally
- **2026 baseline forecast: US$148 billion**; peak-loss scenario: **US$320 billion**
- **Extreme scenario:** peak-year losses could reach **US$400 billion by 2030** (Swiss Re sigma 1/2026)
