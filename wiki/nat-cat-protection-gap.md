# Nat Cat Protection Gap & Financial Stability

> The widening gap between economic losses from natural catastrophes and insured losses is concentrating risk on sovereign and banking balance sheets — a systemic financial stability concern.

## Overview

The protection gap (difference between total economic losses and insured losses) means climate losses are increasingly absorbed by governments, businesses, and households rather than insurers. This shifts catastrophe risk from the private insurance sector to sovereign balance sheets.

## Key Facts

- **Global protection gap in 2025: ~49%** of nat cat economic losses remained uninsured
- **IAIS Nov 2025 GIMAR special:** Uninsurance of nat cat losses concentrating risk on banking and sovereign balance sheets, especially in EMDE countries — systemic implications
- **FSB Climate Vulnerability Analysis Framework & Toolkit** (Jan 2025): foundational tool for cross-border climate risk assessment
- **EIOPA emerging as significant voice** in EU natcat risk pool debate — pan-European pool could share catastrophe risk across borders
- **Africa adaptation finance gap:** <$14B/year received vs. $100B+ needed; credit-rating methodologies blocking access
