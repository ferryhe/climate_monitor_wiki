# FSB — Climate-Related Financial Risks Roadmap

> The Financial Stability Board coordinates international climate financial risk standards, with the Climate Vulnerability Analysis Framework as a key 2025 output.

## Overview

The FSB's Climate-related Financial Risks Roadmap (2021) coordinates international efforts across four pillars: disclosure, data gaps, vulnerability analysis, and regulatory practices. The transition from TCFD to ISSB standards is central to this effort.

## Key Outputs (2025)

- **Climate Vulnerability Analysis Framework & Toolkit** (January 2025): Foundational tool for cross-border climate risk assessment
- **Transition Plans Progress Report**: Companies' climate transition planning disclosure

## Four Pillars

1. **Disclosure** — TCFD → ISSB transition (IFRS S2 effective 2027)
2. **Data Gaps** — bridging climate data availability for financial institutions
3. **Vulnerability Analysis** — framework for assessing systemic exposures
4. **Regulatory Practices** — coordinating supervisor approaches globally
